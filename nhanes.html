NHANES BMI–Diabetes Study

Author: Ulises Bustos

Dataset: CDC National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES)

Research Focus

Examined whether obesity is associated with a higher prevalence of diabetes among U.S. adults using the CDC NHANES dataset. Conducted data cleaning, descriptive analysis, and hypothesis testing in R.

Methods

  • Filtered data for adults (≥18 years).
  • Grouped participants into Normal (18.5–24.9) and Obese (≥30) BMI categories.
  • Applied a two-sample test for equality of proportions to compare diabetes prevalence.
  • Visualized BMI distributions and prevalence rates using ggplot2.

Key Results

  • Sample size: N = 4,833 adults
  • Diabetes prevalence: 4.5% (Normal) vs 16.2% (Obese)
  • Chi-square = 166.37, p < 0.001 — statistically significant difference

Conclusion

Obesity was strongly associated with higher diabetes prevalence among U.S. adults. These findings reinforce the importance of weight control interventions to reduce public health risks of diabetes.
