NHANES BMI–Diabetes Study

Author: Ulises Bustos

Dataset: CDC National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES)

Research Focus

Examined whether obesity is associated with a higher prevalence of diabetes among U.S. adults using CDC NHANES data. Conducted descriptive statistics, data visualization, and hypothesis testing using R.

Methods

  • Filtered NHANES data to include adults (≥18 years).
  • Grouped participants into Normal (18.5–24.9) and Obese (≥30) BMI categories.
  • Applied a two-sample test for equality of proportions to evaluate diabetes prevalence.
  • Visualized prevalence and BMI distributions using ggplot2.

Key Results

  • Sample size: N = 4,833 adults.
  • Diabetes prevalence: 4.5% (Normal) vs. 16.2% (Obese).
  • Chi-square = 166.37, p < 0.001 — significant difference in proportions.

Conclusion

Findings support that obesity significantly increases diabetes prevalence among U.S. adults. This analysis demonstrates how public health data and statistical modeling can inform disease prevention strategies.
